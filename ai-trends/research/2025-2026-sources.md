# AI in Commercial Photography 2025/2026: Research Report

Scope: AI image generation/editing in commercial photography for websites and social media (not print/billboard), 2025 to mid-2026, across five categories: Portrait, Product, Food, Lifestyle, Fashion, plus cross-cutting trends. Every claim is either cited (outlet named, with URL) or explicitly flagged as general/uncited commentary. Compiled by a research pass so every claim used on this site traces back to a source.

---

## 1. Portrait

### Trends

1a. AI headshot generators normalizing for corporate/LinkedIn portraits. CNBC (Oct 18, 2025) reported AI headshots are "changing the way job seekers are seen and get hired," with services like InstaHeadshots, PhotoPacksAI, HeadshotPro and Aragon AI producing professional images in minutes for under $50; headshot use is highest among Gen Z and millennials, and Canva has added the feature. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/18/jobs-search-ai-resume-cv-headshots.html), [TechBuzz](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/ai-headshots-flood-linkedin-as-job-market-gets-tougher))

1b. AI-assisted retouching/culling becoming the default pro portrait workflow (AI as pipeline, not replacement). Aftershoot launched AI Retouching in 2025 (skin texture, blemish removal, eyeglass glare, object removal), free until September 2025 then folded into paid plans; by its 2026 update it markets itself as a full cull-edit-retouch-deliver workflow. ([Photofocus](https://photofocus.com/news/aftershoot-introduces-the-early-release-of-ai-retouching-to-streamline-portrait-workflows/), [ePHOTOzine](https://www.ephotozine.com/article/aftershoot-introduces-ai-retouching-feature-for-professional-portraits-with-free-access-until-september-2025-37251), [Fstoppers](https://fstoppers.com/software/aftershoot-just-became-entire-ai-photography-workflow-903026))

1c. Viral consumer AI portrait trends driving social-media portrait aesthetics. Google's "Nano Banana" (Gemini 2.5 Flash Image) fueled the viral 3D-figurine selfie trend and the Bollywood-style "vintage saree" portrait trend on Instagram in September 2025, with strong face consistency as the enabling feature. ([Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/technology/tech-news/google-gemini-ai-photo-generator-nano-banana-saree-edits-safety-concerns-125091500880_1.html), [Free Press Journal](https://www.freepressjournal.in/tech/google-gemini-nano-banana-ai-saree-trend-goes-viral-on-instagram-step-by-step-guide-on-how-to-try-it)) Teaching angle: these consumer trends reset client expectations for what a "portrait" costs and looks like.

1d. Traditional portrait photography repositioning upmarket. Uncited/general commentary echoed across coverage: the open question is whether traditional headshot photography gets "relegated to executive headshots and special occasions." Zenfolio's 2026 survey (see cross-cutting) gives hard displacement data.

### Tools
HeadshotPro, Aragon AI, InstaHeadshots, PhotoPacksAI, Secta.ai, Canva headshots, Aftershoot (retouch/cull), Google Gemini/Nano Banana, ChatGPT (GPT-4o image). Imagen AI is widely used for culling/editing but wasn't concretely surfaced in search; treat as uncited/general.

### Brand/campaign examples
No single brand campaign defines this category; the CNBC job-seeker story is the case study (adoption driven by individuals, not brands). ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/18/jobs-search-ai-resume-cv-headshots.html))

### Controversy/tension
- Authenticity in hiring: recruiters flag overly smooth AI portraits as a deception risk. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/18/jobs-search-ai-resume-cv-headshots.html))
- Privacy/likeness: in the Nano Banana saree trend, a user reported a generated image showing a real mole not visible in the source photo; Google called identifying markers "coincidental." ([Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/technology/tech-news/google-gemini-ai-photo-generator-nano-banana-saree-edits-safety-concerns-125091500880_1.html))
- Labor: direct substitution of the entry-level headshot market is the clearest photographer-displacement story of the five categories.

## 2. Product

### Trends

2a. AI staging/scene generation replacing basic studio shoots for e-commerce. Photoroom and Flair.ai market AI as a studio replacement; industry guides claim cost cuts of 80-95% per image versus $200-$5,000 traditional sessions. ([Photoroom](https://www.photoroom.com/blog/ai-tools-product-photography), [Flair.ai](https://flair.ai/), [Digital Applied 2026 guide](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/ai-product-photography-tools-ecommerce-2026-guide)) A January 2026 blind-test stat (51.3% consumer identification accuracy, attributed to an "E-Commerce Foundation") could not be independently verified, flagged weak.

2b. Marketplace platforms building generation in natively. Amazon's AI Creative Studio (launched Oct 2024, expanded through 2025) turns basic product photos into lifestyle images/video inside Seller Central; Amazon Ads reported ~5% more sales per advertiser for Image Generator users. Google's Product Studio does text-to-image product staging free in Merchant Center and via Shopify. ([Amazon Ads](https://advertising.amazon.com/library/news/unboxed-ai-announcement), [Retail Dive](https://www.retaildive.com/news/google-product-studio-imagery-generative-artificial-intelligence/651113/))

2c. Consistency/catalog-scale workflows over one-off hero images. Photoroom standardizes large SKU catalogs; Flair targets conceptual lifestyle scenes, a useful "production tool vs creative tool" teaching frame. ([Photoroom](https://www.photoroom.com/ai-product-photography/e-commerce))

2d. Editing-model leap makes product compositing prompt-based. Flux.1 Kontext (in-context editing) was added to Photoshop beta's Generative Fill in September 2025; Google's Nano Banana Pro powers image generation inside Google Ads. ([Wikipedia/Flux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flux_(text-to-image_model)), [Google blog](https://blog.google/technology/ai/nano-banana-pro/))

### Tools
Photoroom, Flair.ai, Amazon AI Creative Studio, Google Product Studio, Adobe Firefly/Photoshop Generative Fill (Flux Kontext Pro option), Nano Banana Pro via Google Ads. Pebblely, Recraft, Magnific are relevant per general knowledge but unverified this session.

### Brand/campaign examples
No named DTC brand campaign surfaced with verifiable results; the strongest citable case is Amazon's own ~5% sales-lift figure. ([Amazon Ads](https://advertising.amazon.com/library/news/unboxed-ai-announcement))

### Controversy/tension
- Product truthfulness: AI scenes risk misrepresenting scale, texture, color of physical goods.
- Labor: commoditized packshot work is the most automated segment; product photographers repositioning as CGI/AI art directors.

## 3. Food

### Trends

3a. Delivery platforms deploying AI photo enhancement at scale. Uber Eats announced AI tools (July 2025 Merchant Impact Report) that enhance low-quality food photos, even re-plating takeout-box shots onto dishes, plus AI menu descriptions. DoorDash launched similar AI photo tools in April 2025. ([PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/artificial-intelligence-2/2025/uber-eats-debuts-ai-features-for-restaurants/), [Today.com](https://www.today.com/food/news/food-delivery-ai-photos-rcna259748))

3b. Enhancement vs generation is the fault line. Improving real photos is broadly tolerated; generating dishes from scratch triggers false-advertising accusations from customers on social media. ([Today.com](https://www.today.com/food/news/food-delivery-ai-photos-rcna259748))

3c. Dedicated AI food-photo SaaS for restaurants. A cluster of niche tools (FoodShot AI, FoodPhoto.ai, MenuPhotoAI) sells generated/enhanced menu imagery to restaurants that can't afford shoots. ([FoodShot](https://foodshot.ai/), [Photoroom](https://www.photoroom.com/blog/ai-product-images-food-delivery))

3d. Fully AI-generated restaurant advertising arriving. Restaurant Business reported Teriyaki Madness launched an ad campaign created almost entirely with AI (actors, backdrops, voiceover generated; the food itself real), a claimed fast-casual first. ([Restaurant Business](https://www.restaurantbusinessonline.com/marketing/here-come-ai-generated-restaurant-ads))

3e. Stock food photography collapse claim. A single non-mainstream source claims zero traditional food photographs made Adobe Stock top-seller lists Apr-Aug 2025, ~91% AI-made, flagged weak, single-source. ([Microstock Insights](https://medium.com/@microstockinsights/food-photography-is-dead-91-now-made-by-ai-part-2-premium-analysis-d6845b979c14))

### Tools
Uber Eats and DoorDash built-in enhancement, FoodShot AI / FoodPhoto.ai / MenuPhotoAI, Photoroom, Typeface ([Typeface blog](https://www.typeface.ai/blog/ai-image-generation-for-food-and-beverage-brands-how-to-ace-food-photography-with-ai)), general models (Midjourney, Nano Banana, Firefly) as commodity generators.

### Brand/campaign examples
- Forkable (late 2025): SF catering platform quietly replaced real restaurant photos with AI images sitewide without telling restaurants; owners found out post-launch; co-founder admitted they "moved too quickly" and reverted to real photography. ([Today.com](https://www.today.com/food/news/food-delivery-ai-photos-rcna259748))
- Burger King "Million Dollar Whopper" and Heinz's "AI Ketchup" campaign as brand-side examples of AI-generated food imagery in marketing. ([StoryChief roundup](https://storychief.io/blog/ai-marketing-campaigns))

### Controversy/tension
- False advertising: customers calling out generated menu photos as misrepresentation. ([Today.com](https://www.today.com/food/news/food-delivery-ai-photos-rcna259748))
- Platform authenticity rules cutting both ways: Uber Eats asks customers not to submit AI-generated photos to its community-photo program; DoorDash permanently banned a driver in December 2025 for AI-faked proof-of-delivery photos. ([Today.com](https://www.today.com/food/news/food-delivery-ai-photos-rcna259748))
- Ecosystem displacement: food stylists, prop stylists and suppliers affected alongside photographers; high-end editorial/cookbook work still human-led. ([Harrison Todorov analysis](https://www.harrisontodorov.com/blog/ai-generated-images-in-the-food-and-beverage-industry-a-cautionary-analysis/))

## 4. Lifestyle

### Trends

4a. AI eats generic lifestyle stock. Getty and Shutterstock announced a ~$3.7B merger in January 2025, widely read as consolidation under AI pressure; both are building AI capabilities (pose variation, background extension) while offering legal indemnity open generators can't. ([London Daily](https://londondaily.com/getty-and-shutterstock-unite-in-ai-driven-era-a-new-chapter-for-stock-photography), [Foster Fletcher](https://fosterfletcher.com/under-siege-can-getty-and-shutterstock-survive-the-rise-of-generative-ai/))

4b. AI UGC-style ads. Brands generate "authentic-looking" UGC-style creatives at scale for TikTok/Instagram/Meta ads; hybrid workflows (AI variants of top-performing human UGC) are standard in performance marketing. ([inBeat Agency](https://inbeat.agency/blog/ai-ugc-ads))

4c. Virtual influencer peak-then-correction. Second-hand figures (verify before quoting): a 2025 HypeAuditor report claimed virtual influencer posts get ~3x engagement of human equivalents, but brand partnerships with virtual influencers reportedly fell nearly 30% during 2025. ([Stormy AI](https://stormy.ai/blog/ai-influencer-marketing-trends-2025), [Sophie Bakalar Substack](https://sophiebakalar.substack.com/p/what-if-consumers-hate-ai-content))

4d. The authenticity counter-trend. Stock buyers and social audiences shifting toward unpolished, UGC-looking, "real" imagery precisely because AI-polish is now cheap. ([Sophie Bakalar Substack](https://sophiebakalar.substack.com/p/what-if-consumers-hate-ai-content))

### Tools
Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, GPT-image for lifestyle scene generation, Adobe Firefly, Shutterstock's OpenAI partnership, Getty's licensed generator. Krea and Higgsfield are relevant per general industry knowledge but unverified this session.

### Brand/campaign examples
- Coca-Cola holiday campaign (Nov 2025): second consecutive AI-generated Christmas caravan ad; "most talked about" holiday ad on social per Campaign US, but only ~37% positive sentiment; recurring words "soulless," "creepy," "boycott." ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/danidiplacido/2025/11/04/coca-cola-sparks-backlash-with-ai-generated-christmas-ad-again/), [Campaign US](https://www.campaignlive.com/article/coca-colas-ai-christmas-ad-most-talked-about-social/1940473))

### Controversy/tension
- "AI slop" platform crisis: Instagram head Adam Mosseri faced creator backlash over failing to protect feeds from AI slop in 2025; Instagram reportedly adjusted ranking against highly polished synthetic content; Pinterest shipped user-facing AI-content filters. ([Hello Partner](https://hellopartner.com/2026/01/02/head-of-instagram-faces-huge-backlash-from-influencers-claiming-the-platform-failed-to-protect-them-against-ai-slop-in-2025/), [TechBuzz](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/pinterest-launches-ai-content-filters-as-slop-backlash-grows))
- Trust erosion: users less likely to share posts when they can't tell real from fake.

## 5. Fashion

### Trends

5a. Digital twins of real models. H&M announced (March 2025, CNN) it would create 30 AI "digital twins" of real models, who retain rights/compensation for their twin's use; first denim campaign images released July 2025, each labeled "digital twin" in-image. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/03/28/style/h-and-m-ai-models-intl-scli/index.html), [PetaPixel](https://petapixel.com/2025/07/08/hm-unveils-ai-generated-models-that-wont-replace-photography/))

5b. Fully AI-generated campaigns. Mango ran an entirely AI-generated campaign ("Sunset Dream," teen line) and drew "false advertising"/job-loss criticism. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/03/28/style/h-and-m-ai-models-intl-scli/index.html))

5c. AI on-model imagery for e-commerce SMBs. Botika turns flat lays/packshots into on-model photos; $8M seed (Jan 2025), claims 90% photoshoot cost reduction, thousands of brands, Shopify app. ([PRWeb](https://www.prweb.com/releases/botika-transforms-fashion-photography-with-launch-of-ai-generated-fashion-model-mobile-app-secures-8-million-in-seed-funding-302352368.html))

5d. Consumer-side virtual try-on going mainstream. Google launched Doppl (June 26, 2025, Google Labs), try-on in Google Shopping/Search (July 2025), expanded to shoes and more countries (Oct 2025), selfie-based try-on (Dec 2025). ([Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/ai/googles-new-ai-app-doppl-lets-you-try-on-outfits-virtually-120014003.html), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/11/googles-ai-try-on-feature-for-clothes-now-works-with-just-a-selfie/))

5e. AI at platform scale inside fashion retailers. Zalando: AI-generated visuals made up ~70% of its editorial campaign imagery, production time cut from 6-8 weeks to 3-4 days, costs down ~90%, built on digital-twin work with ORENDT Studios. ([Yahoo Finance/Reuters](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/zalando-uses-ai-speed-marketing-070427441.html))

### Tools
Botika, Zalando/ORENDT digital-twin pipeline, Google Doppl and Shopping try-on. Lalaland.ai and Veesual are known players but unverified this session.

### Brand/campaign examples
- Guess in Vogue US, August 2025 issue: ad with two AI-generated models by London agency Seraphinne Vallora; tiny "Produced by Seraphinne Vallora on AI" disclosure; major reader backlash over near-invisible labeling and photorealism. Workflow still involved photographing a real model in Guess clothing for a week as reference. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/07/31/style/vogue-ai-models-guess-campaign), [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/moinroberts-islam/2025/07/29/vogue-erupts-ai-generated-models-spark-reader-fury-and-industry-panic/))
- H&M digital-twin denim campaign, July 2025 (see 5a).

### Controversy/tension
- Job displacement beyond models: critics note photographers, MUAs, hair stylists, stylists all lose work when campaigns go synthetic. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/03/28/style/h-and-m-ai-models-intl-scli/index.html))
- Disclosure size/placement: the Guess/Vogue case is the canonical "technically disclosed, effectively hidden" example. ([DesignRush](https://news.designrush.com/guess-faces-backlash-over-ai-models-in-vogue-campaign))
- Model likeness rights becoming law: New York Fashion Workers Act, effective June 19, 2025: clients must obtain clear written consent before creating/using a model's digital replica; damages up to 300% for willful violations. (See [NY DOL FAQ](https://dol.ny.gov/new-york-state-fashion-workers-act-faqs), [The Fashion Law](https://www.thefashionlaw.com/the-grace-period-is-over-for-new-yorks-fashion-workers-act/))
- Body-image concerns about idealized synthetic models.

## 6. Cross-cutting trends

### 6.1 Platform policies and AI labeling
- Meta: "AI Info"/"Made with AI" labels driven by C2PA/IPTC metadata detection plus a manual self-disclosure toggle. ([Meta Transparency Center](https://transparency.meta.com/governance/tracking-impact/labeling-ai-content/))
- TikTok: integrated C2PA Content Credentials, requires user labeling of synthetic/manipulated content. ([Billo](https://billo.app/blog/ai-labeling/))
- Pinterest: user-facing AI-content filters and per-pin AI controls. ([TechBuzz](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/pinterest-launches-ai-content-filters-as-slop-backlash-grows))
- Practical takeaway: C2PA Content Credentials (embedded by Adobe, Google, OpenAI outputs) are becoming the de facto labeling pipeline; stripping metadata is increasingly a policy violation rather than a loophole.

### 6.2 Regulation
- EU AI Act Article 50 transparency obligations apply from August 2, 2026: machine-readable marking of generative outputs, deepfake disclosure at first exposure. ([EU AI Act explorer](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50/))
- Getty Images v Stability AI (UK High Court, Nov 4, 2025): Getty's secondary copyright claim rejected; primary training claims dropped for jurisdictional reasons; only narrow trademark findings. UK legality of scraping images for training remains unresolved. ([Latham & Watkins](https://www.lw.com/en/insights/getty-images-v-stability-ai-english-high-court-rejects-secondary-copyright-claim))
- NY Fashion Workers Act (June 19, 2025): first US likeness-consent law aimed at commercial imagery (see Fashion section).

### 6.3 Industry sentiment
- Zenfolio 2025 State of the Photography Industry (4,500+ photographers, 70+ countries, April 2025): 32.2% have AI in their regular workflow; only 11.6% negative on AI; dominant uses are selection/masking, background removal, content-aware fill, noise reduction, culling, social-media copywriting. ([Zenfolio](https://zenfolio.com/blog/state-of-the-photography-industry-2025/), [PetaPixel](https://petapixel.com/2025/04/17/2025-state-of-photography-more-pros-are-using-smartphones-and-drones/))
- Zenfolio 2026 survey via PetaPixel (Jan 30, 2026): 58% of photographers report having lost work to generative AI, a sharp shift from 2025's "AI as productivity tool" tone. ([PetaPixel](https://petapixel.com/2026/01/30/58-of-photographers-have-lost-work-to-generative-ai-survey/))

### 6.5 Synthesis
1. 2025 was the year AI imagery went from experiment to infrastructure (built into Google Ads, Amazon Seller Central, Uber Eats, Photoshop, Shopify apps), while also the year public backlash got teeth (Guess/Vogue, Coca-Cola, AI slop, Pinterest filters).
2. Stable pattern across all five categories: low-budget, high-volume, catalog/menu/headshot work is being absorbed by AI first; high-concept, campaign, editorial and identity work remains human-led but increasingly AI-assisted.
3. Disclosure is moving from voluntary ethics to legal/platform requirement (C2PA labeling, EU AI Act Aug 2026, NY Fashion Workers Act), with Guess/Vogue as the cautionary tale and H&M's in-image "digital twin" label as the better-practice example.
4. The authenticity premium is the photographer's counter-position: platforms and audiences are actively rewarding verifiably real, unpolished imagery.
